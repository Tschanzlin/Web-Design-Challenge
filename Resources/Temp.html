Latitude vs. Tempurature

The data exhibits a strong correlation between latitude and tempurature as you would expect. The data exhibits a non-linear relationship beteween Latitude and Tempurature, with more pronounced changes in tempuratures outside of +/- 20 degrees Latitude (approximate slope of +/-1 vs a slope closer to 0 within +/- 20 degrees Latitude).

The preponderance of Northern Hemisphere cities in the data set is clearly visible in the graph. While the data appears to be more strongly correlated with the Northern Hemisphere (positive degress of latitude), this is due to more northern hemisphere cities from the citypy data pull. According to Wikipedia, the Northern Hemisphere has most of the earth's landmass and 90% of the population.
